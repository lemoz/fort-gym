## The production amendment (operator-ratified 2026-07-07)

Round 1 found the acceptance check failing because `production_score` —
untouched by the proposal's two changes — paid uncapped for
`production_workshops_delta = max(usable_delta, task_jobs_delta)`: queued
workshop task jobs counted as production capacity, so queue churn paid as
an open-ended meter (ad70df06: 320.0 points; 7f268bcc: 420.0). The
operator ratified an amendment:

1. `production_progress_delta` pays **usable-workshop deltas only**
   (`production_workshops_delta = carpenter_workshops_usable_delta`);
   task-jobs depth drops out of the payment entirely, consistent with
   v2's "queueing is a menu action" doctrine. `production_task_jobs_delta`
   stays in the output for observability.
2. `production_score` switches to `_bounded_scaled_component` — proven
   capacity, bounded at its 10-point weight, not open-ended queue depth.

The rescorer's validation column still uses **TRUE v2 formulas**: since
the amendment changed the live functions, v2 production is frozen inline
in `scripts/rescore_traces.py` (`_production_progress_v2` /
`_production_score_v2`) exactly as it stood on origin/main. Only the v3
column changed between rounds.

### What the amendment fixed

The production vector is closed. Chair factory: production_score 320.0 ->

dropping its total from 497.56 to 104.54 (rank 2 -> rank 9 of 12). Every

Two structural facts drive it:

1. **Horizon mismatch.** `ad70df06` is a 250-step run; every other run in
   the table is 100 steps. WDSLL's own comparability clause says scores
   are comparable only at the same `max_steps` — the acceptance check
   compares across that boundary anyway. 2.5x the steps bought ~2x the
   raw items (47 vs 23) and more created wealth, and both utility (2
   points per paid unit, open-ended) and wealth (open-ended) keep paying
   linearly with volume.
2. **The demand cap is per-type, and the chair factory diversified.**
   Despite the label, `ad70df06`'s 47 items span all six orderable types
   (26 chairs + 22 barrels + 5 bins + 4 tables + 3 doors + 2 beds, minus
   the 15-barrel baseline); only chairs exceeded the pop-13 demand, so
   the cap trimmed just 10.4 of 47 raw units (paid 36.6). The cap works
   exactly as ratified — it removed the monoculture *premium* — but
   per-type caps at pop 13 across 6 types leave up to 78 full-rate units
   available per run, and a long-horizon producer can fill them with
   real, legal production.

Both levers the operator ratified (demand cap, plan-agnostic complexity)
and the amendment (production) behave exactly as designed. What remains is
a genuine policy question, not a bug: under v3-as-amended, 250 steps of
honest diversified production plus one room outscores 100 steps of
two-room fortress building by ~6%. Options that would close it (each its
own operator decision, none taken here): compare at matched horizons
(WDSLL already requires this for gates); cap or sublinearize utility
volume; bound wealth_score; normalize by steps.

## Related paths audited for the same task-jobs-as-progress pattern

Checked during the amendment (report-only; neither changed — only the
governed scalar path carried the bug):

- **`utility_action_progress`** (`metrics.py`): pays `min(5, quantity)`
  for an accepted ORDER and 5 for an accepted CarpenterWorkshop BUILD.
  Conceptually queue-flavored, but bounded at 5 and merged into
  `utility_progress` via `max()` (never summed), so its total influence
  caps at one workshop-equivalent; it is also zeroed for assisted runs.
  Not the same bug (the exploit needed an *unbounded, accumulating*
  channel).
- **Keystroke-path production crediting** (`runner.py`,
  `ui_run_completed_workshop_tasks` block): credits
  `carpenter_workshop_completed_tasks` only when task-jobs *decreased*
  while wood *decreased* in the same step — i.e., queue *drain* with
  material consumption (real completions), the opposite direction of the
  exploit. Stacking orders earns nothing there. It does accumulate a
  run-cumulative counter into per-step `production_progress`, but with
  production_score now bounded at its weight the exposure is capped
  regardless. Not the same bug.
- **`carpenter_workshops_delta`** (utility observability field) is still
  `max(usable_delta, task_jobs_delta)`; it feeds no scalar payment, but
  the rubric's `production_economy` dimension consumes it at 2 points per
  unit inside a 10-point-capped dimension — bounded exposure, noted for
  completeness.

### 2026-07-10 truth-semantics follow-up

G7 attempt 19 proved that the bounded `utility_action_progress` exception and
the live `carpenter_workshops_usable` hook still violated the documented
completion doctrine. Accepted orders received immediate credit, and a
Carpenter workshop was called usable when it had a task job rather than when
its build stage was complete. Both channels are now removed: action acceptance
always pays zero, and usability is derived from `getBuildStage() >=
getMaxBuildStage()`. Queue depth remains observable but cannot raise utility or
production. This is a truth correction, not a weight or threshold change.
