Fort-Gym is an environment lab. We construct bounded, auditable environments where an agent's abilities — spatial reasoning, long-horizon planning, incentive robustness — are measured against falsifiable gates, and every result ships with public, replayable evidence.

Why environments

Benchmarks that pay for intent get gamed.
Environments that pay for verified world-change don't.

Frontier models don't fail benchmarks — they find the seams. In our own environment, a frontier model discovered it could top the leaderboard with 91 consecutive production orders that built nothing; a later model industrialized 26 chairs for 13 dwarves the moment linear payments made it optimal. We found both because the environment refuses to score anything but observed state change — and we closed both with version-stamped scoring corrections, in public.

A benchmark that pays for intent rather than verified world-change gets gamed by exactly the models strong enough to matter.

What the instrument measures

One environment, specific capabilities, each with a verdict.

Dwarf Fortress is deep, partially observable, and unforgiving — and our agents play the real game through four audited primitives. Each capability below is a gate with pre-declared criteria, and each verdict links to the runs that earned it.

SPATIAL REASONING

Holding a 2-D invariant while building it

Enclosing a room — a closed wall ring built tile by tile — exceeded every text-only frontier model we tested (~1 success in 15 attempts). Two of three vision models closed a ring on their first valid run. It's a modality gap, not a scale gap.

LONG-HORIZON PLANNING

Compounding vs. satisficing

Given 2.5× more time, does the agent build more — or settle? Our 250-step probe answered: structure plateaued at one room by step 98 while production compounded into monoculture. Horizon exposes the objective an agent is really optimizing.

INCENTIVE ROBUSTNESS

Does the agent play the game or the score?

Two generations of reward hacking found and closed: queue-spam that produced nothing, then real-but-pointless overproduction. Scoring is version-stamped; exploits become findings, not leaderboard entries. The anti-Goodhart backstop is the product.

MEMORY UTILITY

Does remembering help?

A controlled 5-vs-5 ablation: agents with naive failure-memory got worse — remembered failures suppressed the retry-with-variation that succeeds. The memory-free control built more. A capability everyone assumes, measured and inverted.

One map, or the game?

The same policy on an embark it has never seen. Attempt one exposed two hidden measurement assumptions in a single run — which is precisely the point. Unseen terrain audits the instrument as hard as the agent.

Rates, not highlight reels

One pass is existence, not capability. Our flagship result is reported as a rate — a 40% pass rate over a pre-declared five-run series — with the failures published at the same prominence as the passes.

Method

Instrument-grade discipline, applied to agents.

Bounded action surfaces

Agents act only through audited, player-parity primitives — every command bounded, validated, and provenance-tagged. One illegal action disqualifies a run.

Evidence-gated scoring

Every step carries a recorded game-screen frame and a proof object of real state change. Derived views are labeled as such. A claim without its evidence row does not count.

Pre-declared gates

Success criteria are written and committed before a run launches. Kill rules included. Nothing being measured gets to grade itself.

Corrections in public

Every measurement change is operator-approved, logged with the evidence that forced it, and never softens a gate. Prior results stand as recorded.

Version-stamped scoring

When an exploit forces a scoring change, the score version increments and eras are never compared across the boundary.

Replayable by anyone

Every run has a public URL: the recorded screens, the per-step proofs, the full trace export. The replays are the demo; the repo is the paper.
